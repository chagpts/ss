Why the Korean Government Cares About the Dollar

Government & National Pension Fund (NPS)

[Disclaimer: This example is a simplified model for illustrative purposes and may differ from actual currency hedging practices.]

The Korean government can indirectly influence the currency market through the National Pension Service (NPS), which holds a large amount of dollar-denominated overseas assets.

To manage exchange rate risk, the NPS uses currency forward contracts. This strategy focuses on stability, not on maximizing short-term gains.

Forward Contract: Simple Hedging Example

The table below shows how currency hedging works — and why a rising dollar does not mean a real loss for the NPS.

Item Explanation
Spot Exchange Rate (Today) 1 USD = 1,350 KRW
Forward Contract NPS agrees to sell USD and buy KRW in 3 months
Forward Rate (Fixed) 1 USD = 1,330 KRW
Contract Amount USD 1 billion
If USD rises to 1,400 No accounting loss occurs.
However, NPS gives up additional gains → opportunity cost (upside limited).
If USD falls to 1,300 Forward contract protects value.
Downside risk is reduced.

✔ Currency hedging limits upside potential in exchange for downside protection. It is similar to paying an insurance premium for stability.
