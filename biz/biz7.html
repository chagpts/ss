Corporate Reorganization — The 7 Types and Their Tax Consequences

Corporate reorganizations allow businesses to restructure without immediate tax cost, as long as strict statutory requirements are met.

1️⃣ What Is a Reorganization?

A reorganization under IRC §368 is not a sale. It is a continuation of the same investment in a modified corporate form.

Tax law permits nonrecognition only when ownership and business continuity remain.

2️⃣ The Seven Types of Reorganization

Type Name Core Idea
A Statutory merger Target merges into acquirer
B Stock-for-stock Acquirer exchanges voting stock
C Stock-for-assets Assets acquired for stock
D Divisive / acquisitive Spin-offs and split-ups
E Recapitalization Debt–equity restructuring
F Mere change in form Same entity, new shell
G Insolvency Bankruptcy reorganization

3️⃣ Continuity Requirements

  • Continuity of Ownership (COI)
  • Continuity of Business Enterprise (COBE)

Without continuity, the transaction collapses into a taxable sale.

4️⃣ Gain Recognition: The Role of Boot

Reorganizations are generally nonrecognition events. However, boot changes that result.

Boot = cash or non-qualifying property received

Received Tax Result
Stock only No gain recognized
Stock + boot Gain recognized up to boot

5️⃣ Basis of Stock and Securities

Basis carries over to preserve deferred gain.

Item Rule
Stock received Old basis − boot + gain recognized
Securities received Allocated based on FMV

Lower basis today = higher tax tomorrow

6️⃣ Example: Boot and Gain

Item Amount
Old stock basis $100,000
FMV of new stock $180,000
Cash boot received $40,000

Realized gain = $120,000
Recognized gain = $40,000 (boot limit)
New stock basis = $100,000

4️⃣ Carryover Basis (Preserving Deferred Gain)

Nonrecognition treatment works because basis carries over. The deferred gain is embedded in the new asset.

Item Basis Rule
Stock received Old basis − boot + gain recognized
Assets received by corporation Carryover basis from transferor
Holding period Tacks onto prior holding period

Carryover basis ensures that untaxed gain will be recognized in a future taxable disposition.

5️⃣ Transferable Tax Attributes

In addition to basis, certain tax attributes transfer to the successor corporation. These attributes preserve the tax history of the reorganized business.

Tax Attribute Treatment
Net Operating Losses (NOLs) Transferable, subject to §382 limitation
Earnings & Profits (E&P) Carryover to successor corporation
Accounting methods Generally continue post-reorganization
Tax credits Transferable if statutory requirements met

6️⃣ Why Attribute Transfer Matters

  • Prevents artificial loss duplication
  • Maintains consistency in earnings measurement
  • Preserves tax burdens and benefits with the business

7️⃣ Carryover of Tax Attributes

Tax attributes do not disappear. They follow the reorganized entity.

Attribute Treatment
NOLs Carryover (subject to §382)
Earnings & Profits Carryover
Asset basis Carryover
Holding periods Tacked on

8️⃣ Why Reorganizations Matter

  • Facilitate mergers without tax friction
  • Preserve investment continuity
  • Prevent artificial loss recognition
