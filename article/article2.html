De-Escalation Techniques to Diffuse Conflict

6 Key Techniques to Diffuse Conflict

1. Address the Conflict Early

Don’t wait for small issues to become big problems. Talk about them before feelings get too strong.

Source: Verywell Mind

2. Know the Goal

Make sure both people understand what kind of outcome they want. Ask: “How will we know this is solved?”

Source: Verywell Mind

3. Stay Calm

Keep your emotions in check before and during the conversation. Take a break if needed.

Source: Verywell Mind

4. Practice Active Listening

Let the other person speak fully and listen carefully. Take turns talking and really understand what they feel.

Source: Verywell Mind

5. Use “I” Statements

Talk about your feelings without blaming. For example, “I feel upset when…” instead of “You always…”

Source: Verywell Mind

6. Get Help if Needed

If you can’t solve it alone, involve a neutral third person like a mediator or counselor.

Source: Verywell Mind

Extra Tips

  • Respect personal space and use calm body language.
    Source: deescalation-training.com
  • Avoid saying “Calm down” — it can make things worse.
    Source: Reddit
  • Validate feelings even if you disagree with the idea.
    Source: facesandvoicesofrecovery.org
