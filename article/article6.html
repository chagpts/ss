The Trap of Controlling Others

Have you ever wondered why we tend to argue more with our family members than with strangers? We are often polite and patient with people we barely know, yet we easily lose our temper with those we love the most.

Psychologists suggest that this happens because of "closeness-communication bias." Because we are so close to our family, we often feel a sense of ownership or control over them. We unconsciously expect them to think, act, and react exactly like we do.

When they don't meet our expectations, we feel frustrated and try to "fix" or "change" them to match our own standards. This desire to make others more like ourselves is a common trap in close relationships. True intimacy, however, begins when we stop trying to control our loved ones and start accepting their individuality.
