Why the Same Words Feel Different to Different People

In many workplaces, there is a manager who speaks very directly. They are not trying to be rude, but they struggle to adjust their words.

They focus on results, efficiency, and problem-solving. Because of this, they often say things in a blunt way.

Some employees understand this and are not bothered. Others find the same words stressful or discouraging. This is where tension begins, even when no one has bad intentions.

A Workplace Conversation

Manager:
“This report is confusing. You should have organized it better.”

Employee:
“Okay… I’ll revise it.”

The meeting ends, but the employee feels uncomfortable.

Employee (later, to a coworker):
“I know he’s not a bad person. He’s actually helpful sometimes. But the way he talks really bothers me.”

Coworker:
“What do you mean?”

Employee:
“He always sounds angry or disappointed. When he says things like that in front of everyone, it makes me feel small.”

Coworker:
“I don’t think he realizes how it sounds. He talks to everyone the same way.”

Employee:
“I know. That’s what makes it confusing. I don’t think he’s attacking me, but I still feel tense every time.”

A Difficult but Honest Conversation

Manager:
“I noticed you were quiet in the last meeting. Is everything okay?”

Employee:
“Yes, mostly. I just wanted to mention something. I know you’re focused on improving the work, and I appreciate that.”

Employee:
“Sometimes, though, the way feedback comes out feels a bit harsh to me.”

Manager:
“I’m not trying to be harsh. I’m just being direct.”

Employee:
“I understand that. I know you don’t mean it negatively. I just wanted you to know how it feels on my side.”

Manager:
“I didn’t realize it came across that way. I’ll try to be more aware of how I say things.”

The issue is not completely solved, but both sides understand each other a little better.

Discussion Questions

  • Do you sympathize more with the manager or the employee? Why?
  • Do you think the manager is doing something wrong?
  • Do you think the employee is being too sensitive?
  • Why does authority make blunt words feel stronger?
  • How much should people adjust their communication style at work?
  • What small changes could improve this situation?
  • What phrases help explain intention without denying feelings?
  • What kind of communicator are you under pressure?
